Meghalaya

The Abode of Clouds

Overview

Meghalaya, meaning "the abode of clouds," is one of the most beautiful states in northeast India. It is known for its scenic beauty, lush landscapes, and rich tribal culture. With Shillong as its capital, Meghalaya is home to the Khasi, Garo, and Jaintia tribes, each with their unique customs, festivals, and traditions. The state is famous for its waterfalls, caves, and the cleanest village in Asiaâ€”Mawlynnong.

Traditional Attire

The traditional attire of Meghalaya varies among the different tribes. The Khasi women wear a 'Jainsem', a garment made of silk that is draped over the body. The Garo women wear 'Dakmanda', a wrap-around skirt, while the Jaintia women wear the 'Dhara', a gown-like garment. Men in Meghalaya often wear 'Jymphong', a sleeveless coat without a collar, often paired with a turban.

Festivals

Festivals in Meghalaya are celebrated with great enthusiasm and are an integral part of the state's cultural fabric. Some of the prominent festivals include:

  • Shad Suk Mynsiem: A spring festival celebrated by the Khasi people with traditional dance and music.
  • Wangala Festival: A post-harvest festival of the Garo tribe, known for its rhythmic music and dance.
  • Behdienkhlam: A Jaintia festival that seeks divine blessings for a bountiful harvest.

Music & Dance

Music and dance play a significant role in the lives of the people of Meghalaya. The traditional dances, such as the 'Shad Nongkrem' (a Khasi dance) and 'Wangala' (a Garo dance), are performed during festivals. The folk music of Meghalaya often revolves around nature and daily life, and instruments like drums and bamboo flutes are commonly used.

Cuisine

The cuisine of Meghalaya reflects the simplicity and natural flavors of the region. Rice and meat are staples in the diet of the people here. Some popular dishes include:

  • Jadoh: A Khasi dish made of rice cooked with meat, especially pork.
  • Dohneiiong: Pork cooked with black sesame seeds, a favorite among the Khasi people.
  • Pumaloi: Steamed rice flour eaten with meat or vegetables.

Architecture

The architecture of Meghalaya is deeply influenced by the environment. The state is known for its 'Living Root Bridges', which are handmade by the Khasi and Jaintia people by training the roots of trees to form natural bridges over rivers and streams. Traditional houses in Meghalaya are made from bamboo, thatch, and wood, built to withstand the heavy monsoon rains.

Religious Beliefs

The people of Meghalaya follow a variety of religions, with a majority practicing Christianity, introduced by British missionaries in the 19th century. However, indigenous religions like Niam Khasi and Niamtre are still practiced by the Khasi and Jaintia people, emphasizing a deep connection with nature and ancestral spirits.

Tribes

Meghalaya is primarily home to three major tribes:

  • Khasi Tribe: Known for their matrilineal society, the Khasi people are one of the largest tribes in Meghalaya.
  • Garo Tribe: Primarily inhabiting the Garo Hills, this tribe celebrates agriculture and nature through vibrant festivals.
  • Jaintia Tribe: The Jaintia people reside in the Jaintia Hills and are known for their unique festivals and religious beliefs.
