Tropical Paradise in the Bay of Bengal

Overview of Andaman and Nicobar Islands

The Andaman and Nicobar Islands are a group of over 570 islands located in the Bay of Bengal. Known for their pristine beaches, crystal-clear waters, and lush green rainforests, these islands are a paradise for nature lovers and adventure seekers. The capital, Port Blair, serves as the gateway to the islands and is rich in colonial history, evident from landmarks like the Cellular Jail. The islands are home to indigenous tribes, including the Great Andamanese, Onge, Jarwa, and Sentinelese, who have lived in isolation for centuries, preserving their unique way of life.

Traditional Attire

Indigenous Tribes: The attire of the tribes is minimal due to the tropical climate and their traditional ways of life. They often use natural materials like leaves, bark, and shells for clothing and adornments.

Settler Communities: Other residents, including those of Bengali, Tamil, and Telugu descent, wear traditional Indian clothing such as sarees, lungis, and dhotis, influenced by mainland India.

Music and Dance

The music of the Andaman and Nicobar Islands is influenced by various cultures due to the diverse settler communities. Folk songs often reflect the themes of the sea, daily life, and nature.

Dance forms such as the ‘Nicobari Dance’ performed by the Nicobarese tribe are popular. This dance is rhythmic and vibrant, usually performed during festivals and special occasions.

Architecture

Traditional Huts: Indigenous tribes live in huts made from bamboo, wood, and palm leaves, elevated on stilts to protect against high tides and wildlife.

Colonial Influences: Port Blair features colonial architecture like the Cellular Jail, a historical structure that reflects the British era’s impact on the islands.

Festivals

The islands host vibrant festivals such as the Island Tourism Festival in January, showcasing the culture of the islands through performances, exhibitions, and food festivals.

Other significant festivals include the Ongi Dance Festival celebrated by the Onge tribe, and Panguni Uthiram and Mahashivratri celebrated by the Tamil community.

Tribes of Andaman and Nicobar Islands

The major tribes include the Great Andamanese, Onge, Jarwa, Shompen, and the isolated Sentinelese. Each tribe has its own distinct language, customs, and way of life.

The tribes live in isolation, and their cultures are preserved through laws that restrict external contact.
